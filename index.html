About Me

I am a PhD student in the Joint Astronomy Programme (JAP) at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, working under the joint supervision of Dr. Tarun Deep Saini at IISc and Prof. Shiv Sethi at the Raman Research Institute (RRI), Bangalore.

My broad research interests lie in theoretical fundamental physics, particularly Gravitation, Cosmology and the Foundational Aspects of Quantum Theory. Currently, I am working on theoretical and statistical cosmology, exploring topics such as large-scale structures, cosmological tensions, and scalar-tensor theories of gravity. My work involves developing and applying statistical estimators to test cosmological models against observations and extract meaningful insights from data. I am also interested in the applications of machine learning in cosmology.
